Family Name

Rollins is an ancient Norman name that arrived in England after the Norman Conquest of 1066. The name Rollins comes from the Norman given name Rdulphus. This name, which also occurs as Ralf, Rolf, and Raoul, is adapted from the Old French given name Raol. Alternatively, the name could have been a baptismal name as in "the son of Rowland" which is pronouced Rawland and Rolland in Furness and Cumberland, where a large family of Rawlinsons has sprung up, undoubtedly descendants of Rowland through Rawlandson.
